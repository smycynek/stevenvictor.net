Who am I?

I've worked in the Boston area for most of my career. I generally lean towards software development that focuses on engineering, math, and geometry. I recently changed focus from Windows/desktop/static-typed languages to more Linux/bash/scripting development. I unfortunately don't tweet or blog much, so please contact me directly for more info.

I create a lot of toy projects that you can see on this site. They often don't accurately represent what I do in my day job (see LinkedIn for that) -- they are more just to expand my skills and try new things.

Uh, okay, so seriously, who are you?

My dad designed television sets and television broadcast systems for Zenith Electronics for nearly forty years, so I'm obsessed with pixels, aspect ratios, Dolby Vision, and "the soap opera effect."

I collect, shadow-box, and frame antique tools, like compasses, soldering irons, and pocket-knives. I do standup comedy on occasion. I lift weights. I program home lighting systems but haven't bothered with a Nest thermostat. I'm lucky to have my wife and daughter.

One of my favorite books is God's Debris, by Scott Adams. If I could retire early, I think I'd do standup comedy and coach kids on math and programming -- and watch too much Netflix.
