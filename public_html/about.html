Who am I?

I've worked in the Boston area for most of my career. I often lean towards software that focuses on engineering, math, and geometry. Recently, I changed focus from Windows/desktop/static-typed develpment to more Linux/web/scripting development. I've always been very API focused, but API used to be a specialized part of system programming, like COM, but now, with REST, API development is just plain "development", so I've become more of a generalist :)

I create a lot of toy projects that you can see on this site. (Hopefully obviously), they often don't accurately represent what I do in my day job (see LinkedIn for that) -- they are more just to expand my skills and try new things.

Unfortunately, I don't tweet or blog much, and my Instagram is private, so please contact me directly for more info.

Uh, okay, so seriously, who are you?

My dad (The 'Victor' in Steven Victor) designed television sets and television broadcast systems for Zenith Electronics for nearly forty years, so I'm obsessed with pixels, aspect ratios, Dolby Vision, and "the soap opera effect."

I collect, shadow-box, frame, and hang antique tools, like compasses, soldering irons, and pocket-knives. I do standup comedy on occasion. I lift weights. I program home lighting systems but haven't bothered with a Nest thermostat. I'm lucky to have my wife and daughter.

Some of my favorite books are God's Debris, by Scott Adams (yes, he's gone crazy, but it's still a good book), A Random Walk down Wall Street, by Burton Malkiel, and Fundamentals of Graphic Communication by Bertoline et all. If I could retire early, I think I'd do standup comedy and coach kids on math and programming -- and watch too much Netflix.
